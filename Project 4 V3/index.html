UKRAINE'S WAR WITH RUSSIA

1991-2022

IN THIS TIMELINE...

You will learn about the modern conflict between Russia and Ukraine. Although these two countries historically have had many conflicts, the current war is best understood when looking at the events following Ukraine's independence in 1991.

December 1991

Ukraine's Independence

Ukraine became independent as the Soviet Union fell. Over 90% of Ukrainians voted in favor of becoming independent from the Soviet Union. Although Ukraine has been independent for over 30 years, it has not been a peaceful 30 years as Russia continued it's aggression.

December 1994

Budapest Memorandum

The Budapest Memorandum is signed. In this agreement Ukraine transferred all nuclear weapons to Russia, making Ukraine a non-nuclear power. In exchange, the USA, United Kingdom and Russia committed to protecting Ukraine's sovereignty and territory.

November-December 2004

The Orange Revolution

The Orange Revolution overturned election results. In the presidential election between Viktor Yushchenko (pro-Ukraine) and Viktor Yanukovych (pro-Russia) created controversy. Yushchenko was poisoned before the election, but recovered. Yanukovych was declared the winner, but it was determined that the election was fraudulent. Ukrainians flooded the capital, Kyiv, and wore orange, the color of Yushchenko's campaign. The protests were succesful and there was a new election, resulting in Yuschenko becoming president.

April 3, 2008

NATO Membership

Russia opposed Ukrainian NATO membership. During a NATO summit there was a debate about extending a Membership Action Plan (MAP) to Ukraine. Russian President Putin strongle opposed Ukrainian membership and stated that Ukraine "is not even a real nation-state." At the end of the summit, Ukraine was not offered a MAP.

November 2013 - February 2014

Euromaidan Protests

Euromaidan protests caused a change in government. Yanukovych won the presidential election and no fraud was found this time. He won on the promise of getting Ukraine in the European Union. He broke his promise and instead turned to Russia. In response a protest formed at Maidan Square in Kyiv. The protest lasted 3 months and 130 people were killed. At the end Yanukovych fled to Russia and Ukraine was able to hold a new election.

February 2014 - March 2014

Russia takes Crimea

Russia seized Crimea as a reaction to the Euromaidan protests. This annexation caused international outrage and is condemned by the United Nations and the European Union. But there are no direct consequences from the international community. The war officially begun, and years of battling follow.

February 21, 2022

Seperatist Regions Declared Sovereign

Russia recognizes the Ukrainian regions of Dontetsk and Luhansk as sovereign. The regions were over taken by Russian-backed seperatists. Putin sent troops to these regions in order to "keep the peace."

February 24, 2022

Russia's Full-Scale Invasion of Ukraine

Russia launched full-scale invasion of Ukraine. Putin predicted that he would have control over the capital of Kyiv in 48 hours. It has now been over 2 months since this new invasion has begun.
